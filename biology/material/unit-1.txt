Structural Isomers - Same molecular formula but different connectivity (atom order)

Cis- Isomers - Geometric isomer where two groups are on the same side of a double bond

Trans- Isomers - Geometric isomer where two groups are on opposite sides of a double bond

Enantiomers - Molecules that are mirror images but cannot be superimposed

Primary structure - Linear sequence of amino acids written from N to C termini

Secondary structure - Local folding patterns w/hydrogen bonds, either alpha helicies or beta pleated sheets

Tertiary structure - 3D shape of a chain, stabilized by side chain/R-group interactions including hydrogen/ionic bonds, hydrophobic interactions, disulfide bonds

Quaternary structure - arrangement of polypeptide chains into functional proteins

Hydroxyl - OH, polar, hydrophilic, forms hydrogen bonds

Methyl - CH3, nonpolar, hydrophobic, affects gene expression

Carbonyl - C=O, polar, found in aldehydes (end), ketones (middle)

Carboxyl - COOH, polar, acidic (charged COO-)

Amino - NH2, polar, basic (charged NH3+)

Phosphate - OPO3(2-), polar, highly acidic

Sulfhydryl - SH, polar, forms disulfide bridges

Auxins - Stimulate cell elongation, govern phototropism/gravitropism

Cytokinins - Promote cell division (cytokinesis), delay leaf aging

Gibberellins - Trigger stem elongation, fruit growth, seed germination

Abscisic Acid (ABA) - Inhibits growth, maintains seed dormancy, closes stomata

Ethylene - Gaseous hormone that coordinates fruit ripening and falling leaves

Trypsin - Hydrolyzes proteins

Catalase - Hydrolyzes hydrogen peroxide

Amylase - Hydrolyzes starch

Aldolase - Hydrolyzes sugars

Gap junction - Channels that directly connect cytoplasm of adjacent animal cells (synchronized heart contraction)

Desmosomes - Anchoring junctions that link cytoskeletons of adjacent cells (i.e., skin cells resisting stretching)

Tight junction - Junction where plasma membranes of adjacent cells are fused (intestinal cells)

Extracellular matrix - Network of extracellular molecules that provide structural support (collagen, elastin, etc.)

Turgid - Plant cells full of water (healthy)

Plasmolyzed - Plant cells with lost water due to hypertonic osmosis

Crenated - Animal cells that shrink due to water loss

Integral proteins - Lipid bilayer proteins that are usually channels or receptors

Peripheral proteins - Proteins attached to surface of membrane, often for signaling or structural support

Glycoproteins - Proteins with carb chains, important in cell recognition and signaling

Hydrolysis - Uses water to break bonds

Dehydration Synthesis - Forms bonds by removing water

Endocytosis - Cell engulfs material from environment into a vesicle

Phagocytosis - Type of endocytosis, cell engulfs LARGE particles or other cells

Microvilli - Finger-like surface projections on epithelial cells that increase area

Nucleus - Stores DNA and controls cell activities

Nucleolus - Synthesizes rRNA and assembles ribosome subunits

Ribosomes - Synthesizes proteins, can be free (for cellular use) or attached to rough ER (for export)

Mitochondria - Produces ATP through cellular respiration

Rough ER - Synthesizes and folds proteins, studded w/ribosomes

Smooth ER - Synthesizes lipids, detoxifies chemicals, stores calcium ions

Golgi apparatus - Modifies, packages, and transports proteins/lipids in vesiclesâ€”cis face receives from ER, trans face exports

Cytoplasm/Cytosol - Jelly-like fluid that supports organelles

Cell membrane - Semi-permeable barrier that regulates transport and communication

Peroxisomes - Break down fatty acids and neutralize toxic molecules

Cytoskeleton - Maintain cell shape, aids transport

Vesicles - Membrane-bound sacs for transporting molecules in/out the cell

Centrosome - Microtubule-organizing center, contains centrioles in animal cells for division

Cell wall - Provides structure, protection, and prevents over-expansion, PLANT ONLY

Chloroplasts - Conducts photosynthesis, PLANT ONLY

Large central vacuole - Stores water, nutrients, waste, and maintains turgor pressure, PLANT ONLY

Plasmodesmata - Channels that allow transport and communication between plant cells, PLANT ONLY

Centrioles - Organize microtubules to form cell division spindle, ANIMAL ONLY

Lysosomes - Digests macromolecules, damaged organelles, and pathogens, ANIMAL ONLY

Amphipathic - Contains both polar (hydrophilic) and nonpolar (hydrophobic) regions

Peptidoglycan - Polymer of sugars and amino acids, forms bacteria cell walls

Chitin - Polysaccharide that forms fungi cell walls

Cellulose - Beta-glucose polysaccharide found in plant cell walls

Hypertonic - Higher concentration of solutes outside the cell, water rushes outside, cell shrivels

Hypotonic - Lower concentration of solutes outside the cell, water rushes inside, cell swells

Isotonic - Same concentration of solutes inside and outside the cell

Glycosidic Bond - Covalently links monosaccharides

Peptide Bond - Covalently links amino acids

Phosphodiester Bond - Covalently links nucleotides

CAP - Catabolite Activator Protein, enhances expression of glucose-metabolizing genes

dNTP - Deoxyribonucleotide Triphosphate, building blocks of DNA (nitrogenous base, sugar molecule, three phosphate groups)

Phosphatase - Removes phosphate groups

Phosphorylase - Adds phosphate groups

Perforated - Structure/tissue that has holes/openings (i.e., membranes)

Channel Proteins - Proteins that create pores for ions or molecules to passively transport
