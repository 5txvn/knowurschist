**Overview**: Cycle where nitrogen is converted between different forms to make {proteins} and {nucleic acids}

1. {Nitrogen Fixation} - {Atmospheric nitrogen} is converted to {ammonia}. This occurs through biological fixation with {soil/legume root bacteria} or atmospheric fixation where {lightning} strikes {N2}, breaks it apart, and lets it combine with {O2}.
2. {Nitrification} - Soil bacteria convert {ammonia} to {nitrites}, which are then converted to {nitrates}.
3. {Assimilation} - {Plants} absorb nitrogen from {soil} through {roots}, {animals} get their nitrogen {eating plants}
4. {Ammonification} - {Decomposers} break down organic matter from dead plants and animals, {nitrogen} returns to {soil} in the form of {ammonia}
5. {Denitrification} - Specialized {bacteria} convert {nitrates} back to {nitrogen} gas for the atmosphere, completing the loop
