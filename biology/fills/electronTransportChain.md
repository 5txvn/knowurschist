**Overview**: Takes place in the {cristae} (folds) of the {inner mitochondrial membrane}. Purpose is to create a {proton gradient} to create a bunch of ATP thru {ATP Synthase}. Proton gradient is created as electrons pass through {4} complexes.

**Inputs**: {10 NADH}, {2 FADH2}

**Outputs**: {~25-30 ATP} (NADH), {~3-4 ATP} (FADH2)

1. {NADH} arrives at {Complex I}, dropping electrons and turning into {NAD+} (goes back to Glycolysis or Krebs).
2. {FADH2} arrives at {Complex II}, dropping electrons and turning into {FAD}. Because it’s dropped off at {Complex II}, it misses the first pump and thus, generates less energy.
3. Electrons move through the complex like electricity through a wire. They lose energy in the process and this energy is used to pump {protons} from the {matrix} to the {intermembrane space}.
4. {Oxygen} accepts the abundance of electrons at {Complex IV}. This creates {water}.
5. {Protons} flow back into {matrix} through {ATP Synthase}. This literally spins the enzyme and this mechanical energy is used to tag a phosphate onto {ADP} to make {ATP}.
