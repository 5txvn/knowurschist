**Overview**: {Anaerobic} process that occurs entirely in the {cytosol}, used in almost every living organism. 

**Inputs**: Glucose, NAD+, 2 ATP

**Outputs**: 2 Pyruvates, 2 NADH, 4 ATP, 2 H2O

1. {Glucose} is phosphorylated by hexokinase to make Glucose-6-phosphate. This costs {1 ATP}.
2. {Glucose-6-phosphate} is rearranged into {Fructose-6-phosphate}.
3. {Fructose-6-phosphate} is {phosphorylated} by {Phosphofructokinase}. This costs {1 ATP}.
4. This 6-carbon sugar is split into {G3P} and {DHAP} (both 3-carbon), and {DHAP} is converted to {G3P}, netting 2 {G3P}
5. {G3P} is {oxidized}, transferring {electrons} to {NAD+} to make {NADH}. A {phosphate} is also attached to it, making BPG
6. Molecule is unstable so {phosphate} gets dropped onto {ADP} to make {ATP} (2 net since there’s 2 BPG)
7. Phosphate is moved and {water} gets kicked from 3-PG to make high-energy bond, making {PEP}
8. {PEP} and {ADP} make {ATP} and a {Pyruvate}
