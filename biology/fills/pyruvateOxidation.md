**Overview**: {Mitochondrial matrix} stage that connects {Glycolysis} and the {Citric Acid Cycle}. {Pyruvates} are charged so they use Pyruvate Translocase to get inside.

**Inputs**: {2 pyruvate}, {2 NAD+}

**Outputs**: {2 Acetyl-CoA}, {2 NADH}, {2 CO2}

1. 3-carbon {pyruvate} contains {carboxyl} group that gets snapped off to make {2}-carbon {acetyl} group. This {carboxyl} gets released as {carbon dioxide}.
2. {2}-carbon {acetyl} group is {oxidized}, reducing {NAD+} to {NADH}.
3. {2}-carbon {acetyl} group is attached to {Coenzyme A} via high-energy {sulfur} bond, making {Acetyl-CoA}.
